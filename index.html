Why It Matters

What is HHT?

Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia (HHT) is a rare genetic disorder that causes abnormal connections between arteries and veins — called arteriovenous malformations (AVMs) — throughout the body. Left undiagnosed, AVMs in the brain, lungs, and liver can lead to life-threatening complications.

HHT affects approximately 1 in 5,000 people worldwide, yet it remains widely underdiagnosed. The Anzell Lab is working to change that through molecular genetics, cellular biology, and the development of new diagnostic and therapeutic tools.
